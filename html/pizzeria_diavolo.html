Pizzeria Diavolo

Application Design

About the Project

Pizzeria Diavolo, is a unique pizzeria that allows its customers to customize their pizza in any which way. The owner of Pizzeria Diavolo is looking to expand their reach and wants to create a pizza application that will allow their customers to order customized pizzas quickly and efficiently.

The owner of Pizzeria Diavolo wants to create a pizza application that will let users order a customized pizza in under 3 minutes. The application is intended for people who are very short on time.

The final product is a paper wireframe for the owners of Pizzeria Diavolo.

Timeline: 5 weeks

My Role: UX Research and Design

Competitive Analysis

I started by analysing two companies similar to Pizzeria Diavolo to understand their approach to ordering pizza, as well as possible pain points their site has.


User Interview

I conducted two semi-structured interviews to understand the user's perspective of either ordering pizza or ordering food online.

Some of the insights gained were:

  • having the option to pay in store
  • having the option to leave a note
  • having standard pizzas to choose from
  • not having to create an account to order

User Flow

I created a user flow to demonstrate the steps a user takes to order a pizza, this helped me to find possible places that could lengthen the time it takes to order a pizza.


Journey Map

I created a journey map that shows the steps to ordering a pizza, so that I could better understand the motions a user might go through.

Solution

To be able to reach the goal of ordering the pizza in under three minutes, the user has the option to login as a guest. They then only have to fill out the neccessary information (step 10) to identify themselves upon pickup.

Another way the user can save time is by selecting a standard pizza, and asking in the notes to remove the few toppings they dislike. The user also has the option to pay in store, this saves them time by not having to enter in all their credit card information but also allows user without credit cards (such as young students) to be able to order as well.

What I Learned

Although this was a short project (and my first), I have learned a lot. The main thing I learned is how much research is involved in designing an app. From user interviews to competitive analysis, conducting all of this is important to building a user centered application. I have also learned to approximate how long each step of the process takes, so that I can manage my time better for future projects.
